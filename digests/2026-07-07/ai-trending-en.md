## 3. Trend Signal Analysis

**Explosive attention around "agent skills" and system prompt transparency.** The #1 trending project today — `system_prompts_leaks` with +1,378 stars in a single day — reveals the community's deep hunger to understand how frontier AI models are prompted. This isn't just curiosity: developers want to replicate, optimize, and build upon these prompt strategies. Simultaneously, `agent-skills` (+1,112 stars) and `claude-skills` (+610 stars) represent a new paradigm: **agent skills as composable, shareable modules**. The ecosystem is evolving from "one agent to rule them all" to "many specialized skills that agents can load dynamically."

**Cross-platform agent interoperability is a major new direction.** OpenAI's `codex-plugin-cc` (+906 today) allows Codex to be used from Claude Code, while `herdr` (+779 today) multiplexes multiple agents in one terminal. The walled gardens are breaking down — agents and models are becoming interoperable middleware rather than monolithic platforms.

**Privacy-preserving local AI continues to accelerate.** `meetily` (+2,494 today) leads the trending list with its 100% local transcription and summarization. Combined with `karakeep` (self-hosted AI tagging) and `zvec` (in-process vector DB), the trend toward local-first AI that respects user privacy while delivering cloud-level performance is unmistakable.

**Rust is becoming the systems language of choice for AI infrastructure.** Three of today's trending projects are Rust-based (`meetily`, `RuView`, `herdr`), alongside established Rust AI projects like `rig`, `qdarnt`, and `dora-rs`. The memory safety, performance, and WASM compatibility advantages are driving adoption.
