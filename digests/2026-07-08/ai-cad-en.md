## 📚 Research Frontier

- **[ArtisanCAD: An Industrial-Level CAD Agent with Expert-Grounded Knowledge Distillation](http://arxiv.org/abs/2607.05750v1)** — Targets long-horizon procedural modeling with feature dependency management. *Relevance:* Direct evidence that "text-to-CAD" is becoming an academic research problem, not just a demo — likely cited by every competing agent framework this year.
- **[A Decomposition-Based Framework for Joint Optimization and Spatial Packaging of Interconnected Systems with Physical Interactions](http://arxiv.org/abs/2607.06087v1)** — Joint optimization plus spatial packaging with physical coupling. *Relevance:* Core mechanical-engineering problem (think wiring harness, HVAC routing, harness-of-systems design). Candidates for direct integration into tools like FreeCAD Part / Assembly.
- **[Generalized altitudes and their bounds](http://arxiv.org/abs/2607.06187v1)** — Extends the classical vertex-to-opposite-face altitude of a simplex to arbitrary point/face pairs. *Relevance:* Under-the-hood geometry: these quantities underpin quality metrics for B-rep operations, mesh quality, and discretization decisions in CAD kernels.
- **[Lower Bounds for Approximating the Vietoris-Rips Filtration](http://arxiv.org/abs/2607.06524v1)** — Computational-topology lower bounds. *Relevance:* TDA is increasingly used for shape analysis pipelines upstream of CAD/CAM — these bounds clarify what's tractable.
- **[Shifting is Optimal under Gap-ETH: A Lower Bound Framework for Geometric Approximation Schemes](http://arxiv.org/abs/2607.06069v1)** — Tight complexity bounds for geometric PTAS techniques. *Relevance:* Practical guidance for packer, slicer, and simplification algorithms — separates genuinely hard problems from those admitting good approximations.
