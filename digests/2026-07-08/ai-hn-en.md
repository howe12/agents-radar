## 3. Community Sentiment Signal

Today's HN AI threads skew Anthropic-positive in attention but not in affection: the front page is overflowing with Claude product news, yet the highest-engagement items are largely *alternatives* (Rowboat, Shellular) or *critiques* (Ask HN on dysfunction, the AI story-publishing piece). Active threads cluster around three themes: (1) **competitive positioning** — Chinese model cost advantages and the Abnormal lawsuit both suggest the market is consolidating faster than users expected; (2) **tooling lock-in** — the Fable 5 extension reads to many as a pricing-signal prelude, which is why Rowboat's local-first pitch is resonating; (3) **sociotechnical anxiety** — pieces on LLMs making companies dysfunctional, the "Merge" with AI, and AI-authored fiction publishing all signal a community starting to step back from pure build-mode excitement. Compared to the previous cycle, there's a measurable shift from "what can I build?" to "what is this doing to us?" — while developer enthusiasm for shipping remains intact.
